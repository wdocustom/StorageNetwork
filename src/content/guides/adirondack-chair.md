# Low Boy Modern Adirondack Chair — Build Guide

> A sleek, low-profile take on the classic Adirondack. Built from standard dimensional lumber, this chair sits close to the ground with a raked back for all-day comfort. Weekend build, beginner-friendly, built to last outdoors.

### Hardware & Adhesive

- Clamps (at least 2)

- Speed square
- Tape measure and pencil

## Prep Phase — Cut All Angles & Drill All Pocket Holes

Do all cutting and pocket-hole drilling *before* any glue touches the wood. This keeps the work flat and accurate.

### A. Angle Cuts

#### Base (Side Rails) — 38" × 7-1/2"

These carry the entire chair so the angle cuts determine how the chair sits and reclines.
Lay a board flat with the **right side = front** and the **left side = rear**.

**Front angle (right end):**
1. On the right end, mark **4"** up from the bottom edge.
2. Measure **2"** in from the right corner along the top edge.
3. Draw a straight line connecting those two marks.
4. Cut along that line. This creates the forward lean of the seat.

**Rear angle (left end):**
1. On the left end, mark **2-1/2"** up from the bottom edge.
2. Measure **6-1/2"** in from the left corner along the bottom edge.
3. Draw a straight line connecting those two marks.
4. Cut along that line. This sets the recline angle of the back.

Repeat identically for the second base board — they should be mirror images when laid side by side with their inside faces up.

#### Legs — 20-1/4" × 5-1/2"

Both the top and bottom of each leg get a small compound angle cut so they sit flush against the angled base.

- On each end, mark **1-3/8" offset** from the corner and draw a line across the face.
- Cut both ends of both legs along those lines. Cut both legs the same way — they are identical.

#### Back Supports — 17-1/2" × 4-1/4"

These are ripped from 2×6 (5-1/2" wide → **4-1/4" wide**).

- The angled cut is on the **rear end only** (the end that will face the back of the chair).
- The front end is left square.
- Cut both boards, then **flip one board end-for-end** so they form a left/right mirror pair when you set them next to each other. The angled ends should be on opposite sides. This is critical — they are not identical, they are mirrored.

#### Arm Rests — 24-1/4" × 4"

These are ripped from 2×6 (5-1/2" wide → **4" wide**).

- The front end gets a **decorative round-over**. Trace a circle using a 1" diameter washer or hole saw centered on the front corner, then cut to shape with a jig saw.
- **Pre-drill 3 countersunk holes** for the leg attachment. Offset all holes **3/4" from the side edge** so screws bite into the leg face without splitting.

### B. Pocket Holes

Set your pocket-hole jig for **2-1/2" screws**.

Drill pocket holes on the following parts. **Always place pocket holes on the inside (hidden) face** so they won't be visible from the front or sides of the finished chair.

| Part | Where to drill |
|---|---|
| Back Supports | Both ends — inside face |
| All 6 slats (seat + back) | Both ends of each board — inside face |

> **Pro tip:** Before drilling, draw a small arrow on each pocket-hole face pointing toward the center of the chair. This prevents the common mistake of drilling on the wrong side and ending up with visible holes.

## Step-by-Step Assembly

### Step 1 — Base to Back Support

*You're building the main structural "L" of the chair.*

1. Apply a bead of **Titebond III** to the mating surface of one back support.
2. Position it on the rear of the base board, angled end facing back, square end facing front.
3. Clamp in place.
4. Drive **2-1/2" pocket hole screws** through the pocket holes to lock it down.
5. Repeat for the second base + back support — but **flip the back support orientation** so the pocket holes end up on the inside face of the chair. (Both assemblies are mirror images.)

**Check:** Hold both assemblies up together. They should look like a left rail and a right rail. If the pocket holes are facing each other, you're good.

---

### Step 2 — Attach the 3 Back Slats

*The back takes shape here. Consistent spacing is everything.*

1. Stand both base assemblies up, back supports facing you.
2. Start with the **bottom back slat** — it sits directly on the back support shelf. Apply glue to the back support contact points.
3. Drive **2-1/2" pocket hole screws** through the slat ends into the back supports.
4. Grab your **1/4" spacer block**. Place it on top of the first slat, set the second slat on the spacer, glue, and screw.
5. Repeat with the spacer for the third (top) slat.

> **Pro tip:** The spacer ensures even drainage gaps and a consistent, professional look. Don't skip it, even on the last slat.

---

### Step 3 — Attach the 3 Seat Slats

*Flipping the chair upside down makes this much easier and gives you consistent gaps.*

1. **Flip the entire chair assembly upside down** so the base boards face up.
2. The seat slats run front-to-back across the base boards, flush with the outside edges of the base.
3. Start at what will be the **rear of the seat** (closest to the back slats). Apply glue, set the first seat slat, clamp, and drive **2-1/2" pocket hole screws**.
4. Use the **1/4" spacer block** between each seat slat as you work toward the front.
5. The front seat slat should sit **flush with the front edge of the base boards**.

Flip the chair back upright and check your work before the glue sets.

---

### Step 4 — Attach the Legs

*The legs set the chair height and final recline angle.*

1. **Prop the chair on its rear angle** — the chair should want to naturally lean back at this point. Use a scrap board and a clamp to hold it steady at that angle while you work.
2. The legs attach to the **outside face of each base board**, near the front.
3. Apply glue to the leg face that contacts the base. Hold the leg flush to the bottom of the base.
4. Drive **2" deck screws** through the leg face into the base board — pre-drill to avoid splitting.
5. Add one **3" deck screw** from the seat area down through the base into the top of the leg for extra draw.
6. Repeat on the other side. Remove the prop and confirm the chair sits flat and level.

---

### Step 5 — Cut & Attach the Armrests

*The last step — and the one that turns it from lumber into a chair.*

**Cut first (if you haven't already):**
- Rip to **4"** wide on the table saw.
- Trace and cut the **round-over** on the front end with the jig saw.
- Pre-drill **3 countersunk holes** offset **3/4" from the side edge**.

**Attach:**
1. Lay the armrest on top of the leg. The front of the armrest should overhang the leg by a comfortable amount; the rear of the armrest will reach back toward the top back slat.
2. At the **rear of the armrest**, apply glue and drive a **2" lag screw** down through the armrest into the back support. This is the structural anchor.
3. At the **front of the armrest**, drive **3 deck screws** (use the pre-drilled holes) down into the leg for a solid connection.
4. Repeat on the other side.

---

## Final Sanding & Finishing

Sand the entire chair before applying finish:

- **80 grit** to knock off any rough mill marks and break sharp corners
- **120 grit** to smooth everything out
- **180 grit** on the armrests and seat slats (anywhere skin will touch)

**Finishing options (pick one):**

| Finish | Look | Reapply |
|---|---|---|
| Exterior deck oil (Penofin, Cabot) | Natural warm wood tone | Every 1–2 years |
| Solid exterior stain | Rich color, hides grain | Every 2–3 years |
| Exterior paint + primer | Any color, opaque | Every 3–5 years |
| Cetol or spar varnish | Clear protective gloss | Every 1–2 years |

> **Pro tip:** Apply your first coat of finish to all the **individual cut parts before assembly**. Getting finish into the end grain and pocket holes before glue-up dramatically extends the life of the chair in wet climates. Apply a second coat after assembly.

> **Pro tip:** Use **stainless steel** or **hot-dipped galvanized** screws throughout if the chair will live in a coastal environment or wet climate. Standard zinc coatings will rust through in 3–5 seasons.

## Installer Notes

*For professional installation as a bundled offering:*

- **Build time per unit (shop-built):** ~2.5 hours
- **Build time per unit (on-site):** ~3.5 hours (add setup/cleanup)
- **Suggested pair pricing:** Quote chairs as a pair — a single chair rarely sells when a pair is on display.
- **Upsell opportunity:** Matching side table (one 2×8×8 + one 2×6×8 covers materials for the table).
- **Demand trigger:** List this in the configurator as an add-on once a customer books a garage storage unit. "Complete your outdoor space" positioning.
