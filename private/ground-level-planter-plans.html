Storage-Network.app | Assembly Phase 1

Step-by-Step Build

1
Rip Your Corner & Rim Stock
Set your table saw fence and rip 4 full pickets into L-profile pairs: a 2-15/16" long face and the ~2-1/2" short face offcut. Then rip 1 picket down the center to yield two ~2-3/4" strips for the top rim.
2
Glue & Assemble the Corner Legs
Apply Titebond III to the broad face of each long-face strip and clamp the short-face strip into an L. Drive 5 evenly-spaced 1-1/4" brads down the seam of each leg — 4 legs total, 20 brads.
3
Crosscut the Side Wall Pickets
Crosscut 6 pickets to 22-3/4" (A/C — left & right walls) and 6 pickets to 21-1/2" (B/D — front & back walls). Square every end — these tuck behind the legs, so accuracy here is what keeps the corners tight.

Storage-Network.app | Assembly Phase 2

Step-by-Step Build

4
Build the A/C Walls First
Stand two corner legs upright and stack 3 pickets (22-3/4") between them. Glue and drive 6 x 1" brads through each picket end into the leg behind it. Repeat for the opposite A/C wall — you now have two L-shaped wall assemblies.
5
Close the Box with the B/D Walls
Stand the two A/C assemblies up facing each other and tie them together with the 21-1/2" B/D pickets, nailing into the same legs from the adjacent face. Glue and brad every seam — when the box is closed you'll have driven 144 x 1" brads across all 12 side pickets.
6
Assemble & Attach the Top Rim
Lay the two 24" long rim pieces and two 18-1/2" short rim pieces in the "chase-the-tail" layout, glue the butt joints, and nail a 4-brad cluster at each corner (16 total) plus field brads at 3–5" on center. Glue and brad-nail the finished frame to the top edge of the box with 1-1/4" brads.
