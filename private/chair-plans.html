Storage-Network.app | Assembly Phase 1

Step-by-Step Build

1
Drill the Pocket Holes
Set your pocket hole jig for 1.5" material. You will drill holes for 2-1/2" screws:

Back Supports (x2): Drill 3 pocket holes at the bottom edge. Pay strict attention to orientation: holes must face the inside of the chair.

Slats (x6): Drill 2 pocket holes on each end of all 6 slats on the "ugly" face to keep them hidden.
[ PHOTO: Pocket holes drilled ]
2
Attach Support to Base
Apply Titebond III to the bottom of the Back Support.

Align it with the Chair Base (ensure the un-angled flat edge of the support faces the front). Drive 2-1/2" pocket hole screws through the support into the base. Repeat for the opposite side.
[ PHOTO: Support attached to base ]
3
Install the Back Slats
Stand the two Base/Support assemblies upright. Take 3 pre-drilled slats.

Attach them spanning across the Supports using 2-1/2" screws. Place a 1/4" spacer block between each slat as you move down to ensure even gaps.
[ PHOTO: Back slats installed ]

Storage-Network.app | Assembly Phase 2

Step-by-Step Build

4
Install the Seat Slats
Carefully flip the chair upside down.

Apply adhesive and attach the remaining 3 slats to the Base using 2-1/2" screws. Use your 1/4" spacer block again. Ensure the slats sit flush with the top edge of the base.
[ PHOTO: Seat slats installed ]
5
Attach the Legs
Flip the chair back over. Lean the chair backward so it rests naturally on the angled rear cut of the base.

Align the front legs. Apply adhesive, then drive 2" deck screws directly through the face of the leg into the Chair Base.
[ PHOTO: Legs attached ]
6
Finish with Arm Rests
Place the armrests on top of the front legs and rear back supports, leaving a 2" overhang on the front.

Attach the front using 3" deck screws. Secure the rear to the back support using adhesive and a 2" lag screw for heavy-duty structural stability.
[ PHOTO: Arm rests attached ]
